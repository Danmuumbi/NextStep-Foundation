How NextStep Foundation Began

Founded in 2010 by John and Mary Wanjiku after witnessing the struggles of orphaned children in their community, NextStep Foundation began as a small home for five children in Nairobi. What started as a personal mission to provide shelter and education to vulnerable children has grown into a comprehensive support system serving over 50 children annually.

Our founders recognized that orphaned children and those with disabilities faced systemic barriers to education, healthcare, and social integration. They envisioned a place where every child, regardless of circumstance, could access opportunities to thrive.

Today, NextStep operates a residential facility, specialized education programs, and community outreach initiatives that have transformed hundreds of lives. Our holistic approach addresses physical, emotional, and educational needs while fostering independence and self-worth.

Where We Are Today

From our humble beginnings, NextStep has grown into a recognized leader in child welfare and disability inclusion. Our current facility includes dormitories, classrooms, a medical clinic, therapy rooms, and recreational spaces across a 5-acre campus.

We've expanded our programs to include vocational training, talent development, and community outreach that benefits not just our residential children but hundreds more in surrounding communities. Our Walemavu program has become a model for disability inclusion in the region.

Despite our growth, we maintain the family atmosphere that defined our early days. Our children call it "home," our staff are "aunties and uncles," and our alumni remain part of the NextStep family for life.

Our Mission & Vision

Everything we do is guided by our core purpose and our vision for the future we're working to create.

Our Mission

To provide loving homes, quality education, and comprehensive support to orphaned and vulnerable children while championing disability inclusion and talent development to create pathways to independence and success.

Our Vision

A world where every child, regardless of circumstance or ability, has access to love, education, and opportunity to reach their full potential and contribute meaningfully to society.

Our Philosophy

We believe in treating each child as an individual with unique gifts. Our approach combines care with empowerment, helping children heal from trauma while building skills for lifelong success.

Our Core Values

These principles guide every decision we make and every interaction we have.

Compassion

We meet each child where they are with empathy, patience, and unconditional positive regard. Every interaction is an opportunity to heal and affirm worth.

Education

We believe education is the most powerful tool for transformation. We provide academic instruction, life skills, and vocational training tailored to each child's needs.

Inclusion

We celebrate diversity and adapt our programs to ensure children of all abilities can participate fully and develop their unique gifts.

Family

We create a sense of belonging through stable relationships and community. Our children know they are valued members of the NextStep family.

Empowerment

We focus on ability rather than limitation, helping children develop confidence, autonomy, and skills for independent living.

Integrity

We maintain the highest ethical standards in our programs, finances, and operations, ensuring donor funds create maximum impact.
